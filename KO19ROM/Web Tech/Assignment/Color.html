Exploring Our Solar System

The solar system consists of the Sun and all the objects that orbit it, including planets, moons, asteroids, and comets.

The Inner Planets

The inner, rocky planets are Mercury, Venus, Earth, and Mars. They are relatively small and dense.

Earth, our home planet, is unique for its liquid water and life-supporting atmosphere.

The Asteroid Belt lies between Mars and Jupiter and contains millions of rocky fragments.

The outer planets, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune, are gas giants (except Uranus and Neptune which are ice giants) and are much larger than the inner planets.

The Outer Planets

These planets are known for their size, rings (in the case of Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune), and numerous moons.

Beyond Neptune lies the Kuiper Belt, a region containing icy bodies, including dwarf planets like Pluto.

Understanding Our Cosmic Neighborhood

Studying the solar system helps us understand planetary formation and the potential for life beyond Earth.
